Closed & Archived Jobs

A guide to managing your historical hiring data

Overview

As your hiring needs evolve, you will naturally close roles that have been filled or put on hold. Our platform now handles Closed and Archived jobs intelligently to keep your workspace clean while preserving important data.

Key Benefit: By default, the Global Pipeline is clutter-free, showing only active applications. However, you can easily access historical data whenever needed.

Global Pipeline View

When viewing "All Jobs" on your dashboard:

Default Behavior

Applications associated with Closed or Archived jobs are hidden. This ensures your Kanban board reflects only your current workload.

Viewing Closed Jobs

To see applications from past jobs:

  1. Locate the toggle button in the dashboard header (next to "Show Rejected").
  2. Click "Show Closed Jobs".
  3. The board will refresh to include applications from closed jobs.
  4. The button will turn amber to indicate that historical data is being shown.

Per-Job Pipeline View

When you select a specific job from the dropdown menu:

Identifying Closed Jobs

In the Job Selector dropdown, closed jobs are clearly marked with a CLOSED or ARCHIVED badge.

Read-Only Mode

If you select a closed job, the pipeline enters Read-Only Mode:

  • A banner appears at the top: "This job is CLOSED. The pipeline is read-only."
  • Drag-and-Drop is disabled: You cannot move candidates between stages.
  • Candidate Access: You can still click on candidate cards to view their profiles, resumes, and notes.

This prevents accidental changes to historical records while keeping the information accessible for future reference or compliance.

FAQ

Can I still hire a candidate from a closed job?

Technically, the pipeline is frozen. To move a candidate, you should either reopen the job (change status to Published) or add the candidate to a new, active job.

Where do the candidates go?

Candidates remain in the system forever (unless deleted for GDPR). You can always find them in the Talent Pool or by searching for them directly, regardless of the job status.
